London Cholera Outbreak in 1854

The Cholera Outbreak on Broadwick Street, a street in London, during 1854 killed a total of 616 people over the course of approximately 1 month from August 19 to September 29. The Cholera disease was spread through contaiminated water from the Broadwick Street water pump.

The map indicates deaths due to Cholera at various points in London. The red markers are the death counts and the blue markers are the water pumps.
The Cholera incidents in London steadily occurred starting on August 19 with a sudden rise on August 31 peaking on September 1 and September 2. Cases continued to appear at a high rate until September 12 where it eventually slowed down but steadily occurred until September 29.

Naples Cholera Outbreak During Same Time Period

The cholera outbreak on Broadwick Street wasn't the only cholera related incident throughout Europe. The cholera incidents during this time period are part of a broader classification known as the 1846-1860 cholera pandemic or rather, the third cholera pandemic. United Kingdom Census for 1851

Today, the United Kingdom population is roughly 65 million people. Going back to 1851, the United Kingdom had a population about a quarter of its current size with a significantly larger younger age group than the older age groups due to the average life expectancy during those times.

The population of the United Kingdom in 1851 contained more individuals in the younger age groups than the older age groups for both males and females. Noticeably, the population between genders is also roughly equal with more females than males in the adult age groups and less females than males in the minor age groups.
The male population in the United Kingdom during 1851 was made up of roughly a quarter infant to elementary age children, a fifth teenagers, a third young to middle aged adults, and a fifth elderly adults.
The female population in the United Kingdom during 1851 was made up of roughly a quarter infant to elementary age children, a fifth teenagers, a third young to middle aged adults, and a fifth elderly adults.
The total population in the United Kingdom during 1851 was roughly equal between males and females with a slightly higher number of females.
